Crime Summary for Smethwick (2025–Jan 2026)

Crime levels across Smethwick remain consistently high. Violence and Sexual Offences form the largest category with nearly 2,000 recorded incidents. Other frequent categories include Vehicle Crime, Criminal Damage and Arson, Anti‑Social Behaviour, and Theft.

Monthly crime totals show moderate variation but a general upward trend through 2025, followed by a slight dip in January 2026. Forecasting suggests a gradual rise from February 2026 onward, with monthly totals increasing from around 415 to approximately 420 by mid‑summer.

Recurring hotspots include Bearwood Road, Ethel Street, Three Shires Oak Road, and Dunsford Road for violent crime. Vehicle crime is common around shopping areas, parking facilities, Lewisham Road, Richmond Road, and Bridge Street South.

Drug‑related incidents are spread across residential streets such as Adkins Lane, Anderson Road, Capethorn Road, Cranford Street, and Cape Hill. Criminal Damage and Arson cluster around Clent View, Hall Road, Montague Road, Oliver Road, and Grove Lane.

Anti‑Social Behaviour often occurs near transport hubs and on streets including Anderson Road and Adkins Lane, with levels likely to increase in the warmer months. Overall, crime is expected to continue rising slowly through mid‑2026, particularly in violence, vehicle crime, and drug‑related disorder.
